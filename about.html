Australian-born, of Korean heritage, residing in the United States, I wrestle with the multiplicities of my identity.

I am a quantitative analyst - economics and mathematics are my tools. I am an artist - a conductor and violinist in concert halls and crowded streets. I am a traditionalist, a lover of rituals and customs. I am an innovator, a seeker of new modes of expression for the already-existing.

Recently graduated with a degree in Economics and Mathematics, I currently work as a quantitative analyst in the financial services industry. In my off hours, I have been roaming local cafes in search of a good drink and study spot, reading novellas (and finishing some longer books, too), and trying new baking recipes.

Without revealing too much of my identity, here are some of my recent experiences. I hope this list will be ever-evolving.

  • Community Orchestra Assistant Conductor
  • Financial Services Quantitative Analyst (risk management and data analytics pertaining to loan portfolios)
  • University - Academic Teaching Assistant for various quantitative reasoning courses, Research Assistant in economics
  • University - Music Conductor (and the occasional string instrument)
