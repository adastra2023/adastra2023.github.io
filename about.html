Australian-born, of Korean heritage, residing in the United States, I wrestle with the multiplicities of my identity.

I am a quantitative analyst - economics and mathematics are my tools. I am an artist - a conductor and violinist in concert halls and crowded streets. I am a traditionalist, a lover of rituals and customs. I am an innovator, a seeker of new modes of expression for the already-existing.

Recently graduating from Yale University with a degree in Economics and Mathematics, I currently work as a quantitative analyst at PNC Financial Services. In my off hours, I have been roaming New Jersey's cafes in search of a good drink and study spot, reading novellas (and finishing some longer books, too), and trying new baking recipes.

Here are some of my recent experiences. I hope this list will be ever-changing.

  • Doctor's Orchestral Society of New York Assistant Conductor
  • PNC Financial Services Quantitative Analyst
  • Yale University Teaching Assistant for various quantitative reasoning courses
  • Yale Symphony Orchestra Student Conductor
  • Yale University, Department of Economics Research Assistant
